# Level Log — Standard Operating Procedure

Internal SOP for the Rippner **Level Log** app. The app is the shared record of
level decisions (approvals, denials, wrong-class placements) so everyone acts on
the same history instead of a scrolling chat.

Two principles underpin everything below:

- **Look up before you act.** The app opens on **Look up player** on purpose —
  it tells you whether another coach has already approved/denied this player, and
  whether they've been **flagged before** (which drives first- vs. second-occurrence handling).
- **The app never blocks.** Entries always save and it never overrides a colleague.
  Disagreements are reconciled by talking, not by overwriting.

---

## Coach process

### 1. Look up the player first (this is your database)
Before approving, denying, or moving anyone, search the player on the **Look up
player** tab and read:
- **Approval status** per gated level: Approved / Denied / **Disagreement**.
- **Full history** — who logged what, and critically, **whether they've been
  flagged for a wrong-class placement before** (first vs. repeat occurrence).

If another coach already approved or denied this player and you disagree, **talk
to that coach** — don't silently contradict the record.

### 2. Log your decision (Log entry tab)
- Pick your name, the player, and the entry type.
- **Feedback is required** for denials and wrong-class entries.
- **"Showed up at wrong class"** also requires the **level they attended** and the
  **level they should be in**.

### 3. Wrong class — level-gap handling
**If the level gap is ≥ 1.0 and continuing would harm the group experience:**

Within the **first 15 minutes**, check the court sheet for an appropriate-level
class (same time, or within the next few hours).

**If a suitable class exists:**
- Kindly redirect the player to that court.
- Notify the front desk to update attendance.

**If NO appropriate class is available:**
- Tell the player:
  > "I want to make sure you have the best experience. This group isn't the right
  > level today. Please email manager@rippnertennis.com so we can review placement
  > and see if you qualify for a refund."
- Clearly communicate the correct level to the player.
- If this is the **first time flagged** → player is eligible for a refund
  (subject to management review).

### 4. Notify management — same day
- Log the wrong-class entry in the app (this is the record + the email trigger).
- The coach **MUST** notify management the **same day**.

---

## Management process

A notification email fires on every new entry (player, outcome, attended vs.
correct level, coach, feedback, timestamp). Use the **Look up player** tab to
confirm whether this is a first or repeat occurrence before acting.

### First occurrence
- Add a note to the player's profile (external system of record) documenting the
  level concern.
- Send a follow-up email to the player **within 24 hours** of coach notification.
- Review refund eligibility: a credit is issued **only if** the player has **not
  previously been flagged** for registering at an incorrect level **and has never
  received an exception before**.

### Second occurrence
- Add a note to the player's profile documenting the level concern, **clearly
  referencing the repeat occurrence**.
- **Escalate to senior management for approval** before either of the following:
  - Removing the **"Guest Group"** permission from the player's profile.
  - Issuing or sending any formal **Code of Conduct violation** email.
- Send a follow-up email to the player **within 24 hours** of coach notification.

---

## Where notes live

The app is the **alert and shared decision history** — it is append-only and has
no in-app editing. Ongoing player notes (profile notes, refund decisions,
permission changes) are maintained in the **external system of record**. The app
tells you *what happened and whether it has happened before*; the external system
holds the resolution.
